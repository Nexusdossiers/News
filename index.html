Active Shooter Opens Fire from Boat at Southport, NC Restaurant

Southport, North Carolina • Published: July 3, 2024 at 10:15 PM ET
American Fish Company, Southport waterfront

At least three people are dead and eight others injured after a gunman opened fire from a boat into a crowd outside the American Fish Company restaurant in Southport, North Carolina, Saturday night.

According to Southport City Manager Noah Saldo, the shooting occurred just before 9:30 PM ET in the Southport Yacht Basin. Witnesses reported the suspect had been anchored near the waterfront for about an hour before opening fire on patrons gathered outside the popular seafood pub.

After the attack, the suspect fled south along the Intracoastal Waterway toward Oak Island. As of 11:30 PM, no arrests had been made, though authorities confirmed they are questioning a person of interest.

Southport, located about 20 miles south of Wilmington, is a small coastal town known for its quiet marinas and historic downtown. The sudden violence has stunned residents and visitors alike during what is typically a busy summer evening.

Emergency responders rushed to the scene, and all injured victims were transported to area hospitals. The conditions of the wounded remain unknown. A multi-agency manhunt involving local police, the North Carolina State Highway Patrol, and federal partners is now underway.

“This was a deliberate attack on innocent civilians enjoying a night out,” said a spokesperson for the Brunswick County Emergency Operations Center. “We urge the public to avoid the Southport waterfront and report any suspicious watercraft immediately.”

Authorities have not released a motive or confirmed the suspect’s identity. However, they emphasized that the shooter acted alone and used a small vessel to approach the restaurant undetected before opening fire.

This is a rapidly developing situation. Officials ask anyone with information—including dashcam, doorbell, or marine footage from the area between 8:30 PM and 10:00 PM—to contact Southport Police at 910-457-7911.
